## Getting Started

### First Launch

When you first launch FinVault, you'll be presented with an empty dashboard. Here's what to do first:

1. **Set Your Currency**: Go to Settings > Currency to select your preferred currency.
2. **Create Categories**: Go to Settings > Categories to set up categories for your income and expenses.
3. **Add Your First Transaction**: Click the "Add" button on the Transactions page to record your first financial entry.

### Understanding the Interface

FinVault has a clean, intuitive interface with three main sections:

- **Dashboard**: Your financial overview with summaries and visualizations
- **Transactions**: Detailed list of all your financial activities
- **Settings**: Configuration options for customizing your experience

Navigation between these sections is available through the main menu.

## Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard provides a quick snapshot of your financial status:

### Income and Expense Summary

At the top of the dashboard, you'll see two cards showing:
- **Income**: Total money coming in
- **Expenses**: Total money going out

These summaries give you an immediate understanding of your financial balance.

### Savings Tips

Based on your spending patterns, FinVault provides personalized savings tips to help you manage your finances better.

### Recent Transactions

A quick view of your most recent financial activities, allowing you to keep track of recent spending and income.

### Financial Charts

Visual representations of your financial data, including:
- **Income vs. Expenses**: Compare your income and spending over time
- **Expense Breakdown**: See where your money is going by category

## Managing Transactions

The Transactions page is where you'll record and manage all your financial activities.

### Adding a Transaction

1. Click the "Add" button in the Transactions section
2. Select the transaction type (Income or Expense)
3. Enter the amount
4. Select a category
5. Choose the date
6. Add an optional description
7. Click "Add Transaction" to save

### Editing Transactions

1. Find the transaction in the list
2. Click the edit (pencil) icon
3. Modify any details as needed
4. Click "Update Transaction" to save changes

### Deleting Transactions

1. Find the transaction in the list
2. Click the delete (trash) icon
3. Confirm the deletion in the dialog

### Filtering and Searching

- Use the search bar to find specific transactions by description, amount, or category
- Use the filter dropdown to view only income or expense transactions
- Click on column headers to sort transactions by date, amount, category, or type

## Working with Categories

Categories help you organize your finances and understand your spending patterns.

### Default Categories

FinVault comes with some default categories for both income and expenses, such as:

**Income Categories**:
- Salary
- Freelance
- Investments
- Gifts

**Expense Categories**:
- Housing
- Food
- Transportation
- Entertainment
- Utilities

### Creating Custom Categories

1. Go to Settings > Categories
2. Click "Add Category"
3. Enter a name for the category
4. Select the type (Income or Expense)
5. Choose a color (optional)
6. Click "Save"

### Editing or Deleting Categories

1. Find the category in the list
2. Click the edit icon to modify or the delete icon to remove
3. Confirm your changes

## Settings and Preferences

### Currency Settings

1. Go to Settings > Currency
2. Select your preferred currency from the dropdown
3. All financial data will be displayed in the selected currency

### Theme Preferences

1. Go to Settings > Appearance
2. Choose between Light and Dark mode
3. The interface will update immediately to reflect your choice

## Data Management

### Exporting Your Data

1. Go to Settings > Data Management
2. Click "Export Data"
3. Choose a location to save the exported JSON file
4. Your financial data will be saved in a portable format

### Importing Data

1. Go to Settings > Data Management
2. Click "Import Data"
3. Select a previously exported JSON file
4. Confirm the import operation

### Backing Up Your Data

It's recommended to regularly export your data as a backup. Since FinVault stores all data locally, regular backups ensure you don't lose your financial history.

## Privacy and Security

### Local Storage

FinVault stores all your data locally on your device using IndexedDB. This means:
- Your financial information never leaves your device without your explicit action
- No one else can access your data unless they have access to your device
- Your privacy is fully protected

### No Account Required

FinVault doesn't require you to create an account or provide any personal information. You can start using the app immediately without any registration process.

## Tips and Best Practices

### Regular Updates

- **Daily Tracking**: For the most accurate financial picture, try to record transactions daily
- **Weekly Review**: Take time each week to review your finances and ensure all transactions are recorded
- **Monthly Analysis**: At the end of each month, analyze your spending patterns and adjust your budget as needed

### Effective Categorization

- Create specific categories that match your spending habits
- Be consistent in how you categorize similar expenses
- Consider using subcategories for detailed tracking (e.g., "Food - Groceries" vs. "Food - Dining Out")

### Budget Planning

While FinVault doesn't include explicit budgeting features, you can:
- Track your spending in specific categories
- Compare month-to-month expenses
- Use the insights to create and maintain a budget externally

## Troubleshooting

### Data Issues

If you encounter problems with your data:
1. Try exporting your data as a backup
2. Clear the browser cache and reload the application
3. Import your data back if needed

### Performance Considerations

For optimal performance:
- Periodically clean up old or unnecessary transactions
- Use specific search terms when looking for transactions
- Consider archiving older data if your transaction history becomes very large

### Browser Compatibility

FinVault works best on modern browsers like:
- Chrome
- Firefox
- Safari
- Edge

If you encounter issues, try updating your browser to the latest version.
